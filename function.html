Function and Responsibilities of COAL


The Ministry of Coal is responsible for development and exploitation
of coal and lignite reserves in India. The subjects allocated to the Ministry
which include attached and sub-ordinate or other organizations including
PSUs concerned with their subjects under the Government of India
(Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961, as amended from time to time,
are as follows:
1. Exploration and development of coking coal and non-coking coal and lignite deposits in India.
2. All matters relating to production, supply, distribution and prices of coal.
3. Low Temperature carbonization of coal and production of synthetic oil from coal.
4. Administration of the Coal Mines (Conservation and Development) Act, 1974 (28 of 1974).
5. The Coal Mines Provident Fund Organization.
6. The Coal Mines Welfare Organization.
7. Administration of the Coal Mines Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1947 (32 of 1947).
8. Administration of CMSP Act, 2015 (no. 11 of 2015).
